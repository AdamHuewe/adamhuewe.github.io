2026–27 Leadership

Meet the officers leading the Georgetown Law Federalist Society for the 2026–27 academic year.

Executive Board
Sara Ballou
President
3L · J.D. 2027
Sara Ballou
President

Sara serves as President of the Georgetown Law Federalist Society, Senior Editor of the Georgetown Journal of Law and Public Policy, and Visiting Editor of the Harvard Journal of Law and Public Policy. She is also a Research Assistant to Professor Randy Barnett for his forthcoming book, a Bradley Scholar at the Center for the Constitution, and a Bob Horn Fellow at the Republican National Lawyers Association.

Sara spent her 1L summer as a Law Clerk in the office of White House Counsel and her 2L summer as a Summer Associate in Milbank's New York City office. A graduate of Gettysburg College, Sara worked in government, politics, and media prior to law school.

In her free time, Sara enjoys high-altitude mountaineering, playing piano, and learning foreign languages.

Adam Huewe
Executive Vice President
3L · J.D. 2027
Adam Huewe
Executive Vice President

Adam is a 3L and serves as the Executive Vice President of the Georgetown Law Federalist Society. During his time in law school, Adam has worked as a law clerk for the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia, U.S. Senator Rick Scott (R-FL), and Virginia Attorney General Jason Miyares.

Adam is a Blackstone Fellow, an Articles Editor of the Georgetown Journal of Legal Ethics, and a member of the Christian Legal Society. He received his B.A. from the University of Rochester, with a double major in Political Science and History.

Outside of law school, Adam enjoys attending Immanuel Bible Church, weight-training, and spending time with his fiancée.
